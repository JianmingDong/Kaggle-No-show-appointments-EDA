Introduction¶

This report will analyze the dataset of No-show appointments(original download from Kaggle).This dateset collects information from 100k medical appointments in Brazil and is focused on the question of whether or not patients show up for their appointment. A number of characteristics about the patient are included in each row.

This report will analyze what factors are important to know in order to predict if a patient will show up for their scheduled appointment?

More specific, this article will analyze following questions:

  • Does Gender affect whether people show up for the appointment?
  • Does Scholarship affect whether people show up for the appointment?
  • Does Disease type affect whether people show up for the appointment?
  • Does Neighbourhood affect whether people show up for the appointment?
  • Does Age affect whether people show up for the appointment?

Conclusions¶

To summerize, 'Scholarship', 'Disease type', 'Neighbourhood' and 'Age' are the important factors to know in order to predict whether the patient will show up in the appointment. To be more specific:

  • 'Gender' might not be the factor influence whether people show up for the appointment since groups in different gender have same rate of not-show in the appointment;
  • When it comes to 'Scholarship', people who is enrolled in Brasilian welfare program has higher rate of not-show(24%) in the appointment than the other group;
  • Condidering disease type, patient who got Hipertension or Diabetes has lower rate of no-show for the appointment. However, Alcoholism might not influence patient's decision to show-up in an appointment;
  • Different neighbourhoods has different show-up rate;
  • Age could be the factor influence people's behavior, and people who age > 50 has the highest show-up rate for the appointment compared to other two groups.

Limitations¶

  • This article created a new variable 'Age_cat' to divide age into three categories based on common sense, however, there might be a better way for categolize 'Age' by looking at the distribution of the age;
  • This dataset has 110527 appointments in total, however, it only has 62299 patients in total. This means there a 56% repeated patients. If the dataset could be larger, we could be more sure about our findings.
